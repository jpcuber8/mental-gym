# Product Brief

## Product

Mental Gym is a private NCAA cross country mental performance app for Joshua, a UCLA distance runner.

The app should feel like a personal training room: calm, athletic, premium, and specific to cross country. It should not feel like a public wellness SaaS product or a clinical tool.

## Core Goal

Provide a daily 5-10 minute pre-run mental workout that progresses over time like a training plan. The app should normally be used before the day's run, workout, warm-up, race routine, travel-day movement, or recovery block.

The recommendation engine considers:

- current date and season phase
- upcoming key events
- recent check-ins
- weekly adherence
- stress/arousal
- focus
- confidence
- mental freshness
- previous usefulness and execution
- travel, race day, UCLA transition, altitude camp, and championship proximity

## Scientific Positioning

The app is sport-specific psychological skills training. It avoids generic brain games, IQ-style drills, and cognitively fatiguing exercises as the core product.

Core targets:

- arousal regulation
- attention and flexible focus
- motivational self-talk
- race-scenario imagery
- process goals and if-then coping plans
- pre-performance routines
- reset/refocus before the next run when bad workouts, bad races, travel stress, or difficult transitions are still carrying over

The app is science-informed but modest in claims. It is not designed to diagnose or treat mental illness.

## V1 Scope

Included:

- Today dashboard.
- Guided daily session flow.
- Progress trends and history.
- Season plan timeline.
- Mental skill station library.
- Settings with local data export, import, reset, reduced motion, and disclaimer.
- Local-first data persistence.

Not included:

- Supabase.
- Accounts or authentication.
- Coach sharing.
- Admin panels.
- Billing.
- AI APIs.
- Paid APIs.
- Clinical screening.

## Design Direction

Mobile-first, quiet, and focused.

Prioritize:

- real app utility over landing-page copy
- clear touch targets
- compact cards
- readable session scripts
- calm contrast
- athletic language
- low visual clutter
- non-punitive adherence
